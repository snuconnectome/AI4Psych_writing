### Example 3: Annotated Weak Abstract (For Learning)

**Original Abstract (Before Peer Review):**

> Stress is a significant problem affecting many college students. Previous research has investigated the relationship between stress and cognitive performance using various methodologies. **5-Dimension Rubric Scoring:**

```markdown
| Dimension | Score | Justification |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Opening Pattern | **2/5** | Generic problem statement ("Stress is a significant problem"), no clear pattern |
| Broad Significance | **1/5** | Only college mental health researchers would care; no cross-disciplinary appeal |
| Quantitative Results | **2/5** | Vague "significant effects (p<0.05)" with no effect size, CI, or magnitude |
| Explicit Novelty | **1/5** | Generic "contributes to literature" claim; what's actually new is unclear |
| Logical Structure | **3/5** | Standard IMRaD flow, but opening is weak |
| **TOTAL** | **9/25** | **Desk Reject** |
```

**Editor Protocol Applied:**

**Editorial Decision:** â˜‘ Desk Reject

**1-Sentence Justification:**

"Desk reject due to narrow significance (college students only) and absent novelty statement (unclear what's new beyond routine stress-cognition correlation)."

**Strongest Element:** Logical Structure (Score 3)

- The abstract follows standard flow. Not exceptional, but adequate.

**Fatal Flaw:** Broad Significance (Score 1)

- Current: "Implications for college mental health interventions"

- Problem: Only college counselors would care. Nature requires broader appeal.

**Concrete Revision:**

**Current Sentence:**

"Stress is a significant problem affecting many college students. [...] This study contributes to the literature on stress and cognition, with implications for college mental health interventions."

**Suggested Revision:**

"Stress affects 60% of college students worldwide, yet we lack understanding of which cognitive processes are most vulnerableâ€”critical for designing effective interventions across educational and workplace settings. **What Changed:**

1. **Opening**: Problem-driven pattern ("Stress affects 60%... yet we lack understanding...") replaces generic statement

1. **Significance**: Cross-disciplinary (education + workplace, not just college), societal impact (60% worldwide), theoretical importance (challenges existing model)

1. **Quantitative**: 5 metrics added: 34% reduction, Cohen's d=1.2, 95% CI: 0.8-1.6, OR=3.4, p<0.001 (replaces vague "p<0.05")

1. **Novelty**: "We reveal" + "challenging the prevailing model" + "selective vulnerability" (replaces "contributes to literature")
